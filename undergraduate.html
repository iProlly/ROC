Undergraduate Chemistry

Undergraduate chemistry is where you gain a deep and systematic understanding of the subject. The curriculum goes beyond fundamentals into specialized branches, advanced laboratory techniques, and research skills. After this point, you will be able to do a lot of professional works related to chemistry.

Curriculum

The undergraduate chemistry curriculum generally spans four years. The topics varies (quite a lot) by university, but a common structure looks like this:

Year 1: Freshmen year

  • General Chemistry: atomic theory, bonding, thermochemistry, solutions, equilibrium.
  • Mathematics for Chemists: calculus or linear algebra.
  • Physics for Chemists: mechanics, electromagnetism, waves.

Year 2: Sophomore year

  • Organic Chemistry I: structure, stereochemistry, basic functional groups, simple reactions.
  • Inorganic Chemistry I: main-group chemistry, bonding theories.
  • Physical Chemistry I: chemical thermodynamics, quantum chemistry.
  • Analytical Chemistry: advanced stoichiometry, quantitative methods.
  • Fundamentals of Spectroscopy: IR, 1H-NMR, 13C-NMR, UV-Vis, mass spectroscopy.

Year 3: Junior year

  • Organic Chemistry II: advanced reaction mechanisms, spectroscopy, synthesis.
  • Inorganic Chemistry II: transition metals chemistry, organometallics.
  • Physical Chemistry II: chemical kinetics.
  • Instrumental Analysis: chromatography, spectroscopy

Year 4: Senior year

  • Special Topics & Electives: materials chemistry, nanotechnology, environmental chemistry, computational chemistry.
  • Research Project/Thesis: independent research under supervision, literature review, lab work, thesis writing.

Laboratory Skills

Laboratory courses usually go along with lectures throughout undergraduate study. In undergraduate level, laboratory work is more advanced and comprehensive than in high school. You need to be able to use your knowledge to solve problems, to design experiments, and to interpret results critically. Here are some recommended skills and techniques you should master (Of course, this varies largely on university due to availability on instrument):

  • Organic Chemistry Labs: multi-step organic synthesis, recrystallization, purification (column chromatography, rotavap).
  • Inorganic Chemistry Labs: inorganic synthesis, magnetic susceptibility, spectra of coordination compound.
  • Physical Chemistry Labs: calorimetry, reaction kinetics, simulation laboratory.
  • Analytical Chemistry Labs: chromatography (GC, HPLC), atomic absorption spectroscopy, electrochemical analysis, quantitative methods.
