Junior High School Chemistry

Junior high school is the first stage where students are introduced to chemistry as an individual subject. The emphasis is to learn the basic language of chemistry by using some terminology and symbols.

Curriculum

A typical junior high school chemistry Thai's curriculum includes the following themes:

Grade 7 (M.1)

  • Introduction to Chemistry: matter, substances, mixtures, physical vs. chemical changes.
  • States of Matter: solid, liquid, gas, and how particles arrange.
  • Elements, Compounds, and Mixtures: symbols, simple separation methods (filtration, evaporation), chromatography.

Grade 8 (M.2)

  • Atomic Theory (basic): atoms as building blocks, simple atomic model, introduction to elements.
  • Periodic Table (intro): how to look at periodic table, groups, periods, metals, nonmetals.
  • Molecules: idea of ions, molecules, and simple compounds.
  • Acids, Bases, and Salts: basic properties, indicators, neutralization.

Grade 9 (M.3)

  • Chemical Reactions: identifying reactions, simple reactions.
  • Introduction to Stoichiometry: counting atoms, very simple balancing of equations.
  • Everyday Chemistry: examples of chemical reactions in daily life (rusting, combustion, photosynthesis).

Laboratory Skills

Laboratory work at the junior level is about building observational skills, and about questioning critically. Here are some recommended experiments you should try:

Grade 7 (M.1)

  • Lab Safety and Glassware: rules, symbols, handling simple equipment.
  • Separation of Mixtures: filtration of sand/salt, evaporation of salt water.
  • Chromatography: you should at least try paper chromatography once.
  • Observation of Physical and Chemical Changes: melting ice, burning paper, rusting iron.

Grade 8 (M.2)

  • Simple Flame Tests: you should be able to distinguish the elements by their flame.
  • Indicator Tests: litmus paper, universal indicators.
  • Acid + Base Reactions: for example, vinegar and baking soda.

Grade 9 (M.3)

  • Chemical Reactions: combustion of candle, rusting of iron nails, neutralization reactions.
